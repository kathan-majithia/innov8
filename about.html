About this project

Building trust through transparency – a Russia–Brazil nuclear cooperation initiative.

Why Brazil?

Brazil is one of the world’s largest agricultural exporters, yet up to 30% of fruits and vegetables are lost after harvest. A state‑of‑the‑art food irradiation centre in São Paulo can:

  • Extend shelf life of mangoes, coffee, and beef without chemicals.
  • Meet strict phytosanitary requirements of the EU and Asia.
  • Boost farmer incomes and reduce food waste.

Russia–Brazil Cooperation

Rosatom and Brazil have a long‑standing partnership in nuclear medicine, isotope supply, and research. This project extends that collaboration into peaceful non‑power nuclear applications – a direct response to global food security challenges.

Nuclear Infrastructure Elements

Based on IAEA guidelines, this project focuses on:

  • Public acceptance and stakeholder involvement – this traceability website is a core tool.
  • Human resource development – joint training programmes with MEPhI and CNEN.
  • Legal and regulatory framework – licensing an accelerator‑based facility under CNEN.
  • Safety and security – modern electron beam technology with no high‑activity radioactive source.
