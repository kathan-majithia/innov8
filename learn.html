How food irradiation works

The same science that powers space missions keeps your food fresh and safe.

📦

1. Packaged food enters

Already sealed in its final packaging – no re‑contamination possible.

⚡

2. Energy beam passes through

Electron beams or X‑rays break the DNA of bacteria and insects.

✅

3. Food remains unchanged

No chemical residues, no radioactivity – just clean, long‑lasting food.

Common questions

Does irradiated food become radioactive?

No. The energy only passes through the food; it does not leave any residue. The food never touches a radioactive source.

Does irradiation change the taste or nutrition?

At the low doses used for fruits and vegetables, taste and nutrition remain virtually unchanged. Even at higher doses for spices, the effect is far less than cooking.

Is irradiated food safe for everyone?

Yes. The World Health Organization, the IAEA, and over 60 countries have endorsed irradiation as a safe and effective method.

How does this help farmers in Brazil?

It drastically reduces post‑harvest losses – mangoes that used to rot in transit now survive weeks longer without cold storage, reaching new export markets.
